About this project

An independent, open tool for exploring Liberia's census history and projecting where its population is heading.

What it does

The Liberia Census Explorer brings together the results of Liberia's national population censuses — 1962, 1974, 1984, 2008 and 2022 — into one place where you can query them by year, county, sex and age, and project future population with transparent models that run entirely in your browser.

Everything is computed client-side from a single open dataset. There is no server, no tracking, and no account required.

How the forecasts work

Three standard projection methods are shown side by side so you can compare them:

Growth-rate (CAGR)

Compound annual growth between the first and last census, projected forward. The standard demographic approach.

Linear trend

A least-squares straight line through every census — a fixed number of people added per year.

Exponential fit

A least-squares exponential through every census, capturing compounding informed by all data points.

County forecasts use the compound growth rate between the 2008 and 2022 censuses. Projections are illustrative — real population growth is shaped by fertility, mortality and migration that no simple curve can fully capture.

Data & sources
Limitations. The 1984 final report was largely lost during the civil war. County, sex and age breakdowns are only available on consistent boundaries for 2008 and 2022. The population pyramid uses UN World Population Prospects estimates, which differ from the enumerated census counts and are labelled as estimates throughout. This is an educational project and not an official government source.
Built with

The entire interface is built with tan-compose — a tiny, zero-dependency Web Components library — using its kit components and icons packages. No frameworks, no build step.
